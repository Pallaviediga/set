Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a world-famous monument located in Agra, India. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. The construction began in 1632 and took about 22 years to complete. It is made of white marble and beautifully decorated with carvings and inlay work. The monument is a perfect example of Mughal architecture, combining Islamic, Persian, and Indian styles. The Taj Mahal is surrounded by lush gardens, a reflecting pool, and four minarets. It is considered a symbol of eternal love and devotion. Every year, millions of tourists from around the world visit this UNESCO World Heritage Site. The monument looks especially stunning at sunrise and under the moonlight. The Taj Mahal is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and a pride of India.
