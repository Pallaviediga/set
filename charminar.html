Char Minar

Charminar is a famous historical monument located in the heart of Hyderabad, Telangana. It was built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah. The structure was constructed to commemorate the end of a deadly plague in the city. The name 'Charminar' means 'Four Minarets' in Urdu. Each minaret is 56 meters high and has a spiral staircase with 149 steps. The monument is made of granite, limestone, mortar, and marble. Charminar is a perfect example of Indo-Islamic architecture with Persian influences. It has four grand arches facing four different directions. There is a mosque on the top floor that is still used for prayers. It is surrounded by a bustling market known as Laad Bazaar, famous for bangles and pearls. The Charminar is a symbol of Hyderabad's rich history and culture. It attracts thousands of tourists every year from across the world.
